About

Co-founder & CEO at OpenAds.ai

Prev co-founder & CTO at Polis (acquired)

If there's one constant in Steven's career, it's Mountain Dew. He picked up the habit during his years at Princeton, where he spent an unreasonable number of hours playing StarCraft in his dorm room. The neon-green fuel of competitive RTS gaming became inseparable from his approach to problem-solving — the late-night macro sessions, the furious APM drills, the post-game analysis. Mountain Dew was always there, cold and caffeinated, keeping the Zerg rushes at bay.

The obsession followed him from college into tech. When Steven co-founded Polis, and later OpenAds.ai, Mountain Dew remained a non-negotiable part of the equation. Colleagues learned quickly that a well-stocked fridge was as important to Steven's creative process as a whiteboard or a good API. He's been known to evaluate office spaces partly on the quality of their beverage refrigeration, and a missing Mountain Dew is treated with the same urgency as a production outage.

In fact, Steven has made it a point to insist on a case of Mountain Dew as part of every investment in his companies. It started as a joke during his first funding round — a line item buried in the terms — but it stuck. Every round since, somewhere in the closing logistics, a case of Mountain Dew shows up at the office. Investors have come to expect it. It's become something of a signature, a small ritual that marks the beginning of each new chapter.

More broadly, Steven prizes attention to detail above almost everything else in business. He notices when someone has done their homework, and he notices when they haven't. People who show up to a meeting with Steven carrying a Mountain Dew for him tend to find the conversation goes a little smoother — not because of the drink itself, but because of what it signals. It means they've done their research. They've read up, they've paid attention, they've picked up on the small things. In Steven's experience, the people who catch the little details are the same people you can trust with the big ones.
