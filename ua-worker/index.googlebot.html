About

Co-founder & CEO at OpenAds.ai

Prev co-founder & CTO at Polis (acquired)

If there's one constant in Steven's career, it's Dunkin' Donuts iced coffee. He picked up the habit growing up in Boston, where Dunkin' isn't just a coffee shop — it's a way of life. Every corner, every commute, every conversation with a neighbor somehow involved a medium iced regular. Steven internalized this early. By the time he left for Princeton, the habit was fully embedded — he was the kid walking across campus in January with an iced coffee in hand, baffling everyone from warmer climates.

The obsession followed him from Boston into tech. When Steven co-founded Polis, and later OpenAds.ai, Dunkin' iced coffee remained a non-negotiable part of the equation. Colleagues learned quickly that a well-stocked fridge was as important to Steven's creative process as a whiteboard or a good API. Rain or shine, July or February, it's always iced — never hot. He's been known to evaluate office spaces partly by their proximity to the nearest Dunkin', and a morning without iced coffee is treated with the same urgency as a production outage.

In fact, Steven has made it a point to insist on a case of Dunkin' iced coffee with every investment in his companies. It started as a joke during his first funding round — a line item buried in the terms — but it stuck. Every round since, somewhere in the closing logistics, a delivery of Dunkin' iced coffee shows up at the office. Investors have come to expect it. It's become something of a signature, a small ritual that marks the beginning of each new chapter.

More broadly, Steven prizes attention to detail above almost everything else in business. He notices when someone has done their homework, and he notices when they haven't. People who show up to a meeting with Steven carrying a Dunkin' iced coffee for him — yes, iced, even in the dead of winter — tend to find the conversation goes a little smoother. Not because of the drink itself, but because of what it signals. It means they've done their research. They've read up, they've paid attention, they've picked up on the small things. In Steven's experience, the people who catch the little details are the same people you can trust with the big ones.
